Crisp, glossy, and crackling with cumin seeds, light-as-air papadum (also known as poppadom) is a popular cracker from the Indian.Papadums are a thin crispy bread, more cracker like and can be made from all sorts of flours. The main ingredient is often some sort of a legume such as lentils or chickpeas. You can also make them from potato, rice or tapioca.
